ABOUT ME

Ever since I was a kid, I loved math. This led to being introduced to programming in high school with the language 'Basic', with which I immediately hooked. In college, I learned QBasic, Pascal, Assembler, C+, and Visual Basic, with no preference of one over the other.

When I finished college, I began to focus on another branch of computer science, Networking. I started working in a telecommunications company where I learned about routers, switches, access points, fiber optic, and structured cable. When my time with them was over, I began to freelance, which led to a struggle with the consistency of contracts.

Being forced out of my comfort zone, I decided to return to working in the telecommunications sector after about two and a half years away. I would install access control, security cameras, set up the camera servers, paging systems, structure cabling, and some service calls.

One day I saw a banner for a full-stack developer bootcamp and my passion for programming came back!!
